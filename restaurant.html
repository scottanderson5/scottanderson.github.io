Restaurant Re-Design
Time Frame: 2.5 days
(See the project on GitHub or view it live)
Technology Used

RWD, HTML, SCSS, JQuery, Jekyll, Photoshop, Git.

Foundation

Black Sheep Lodge is one of my favorite places to grab some food and watch a game in Austin. Unfortunately, their webpage leaves something to be desired.

As a tribute to a place I love so much, I decided to embark on a re-design of their webpage. This was not for them or for profit, just for my own personal project.

Research

I did a SWOT (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats) analysis. This helped me great a picture of what their key, individual traits were.

I also completed a wordlist so that I could tie together common themes, sights, and feelings from the restaurant.

Design

For the overall look of the site, I created a stile tile which helped me see if my ideas for theming, fonts, and color would work well together or not.

I created mockups in Photoshop as well, so that I could attempt possible layouts.

Coding

I coded the webpage with HTML, SCSS, Jekyll and JQuery. I mixed in a grid system as well, to aid me in overall organization and the webpages responsiveness.

I also only coded out the home page and the menu page, in the interest of time.

Challenges Faced

The menu page was one of the struggles I faced with this project. I went through many different layouts before making a final decision. I also played around with different font-sizes and colors to convey the hierarchy needed on the page. This was to not only describe the individual items, but also the different, broader, categories on the menu page.

I also struggled with how to display the current beers they have on tap. For example, I originally wanted to put the list on the menu page. But after consideration and peer feedback, I put it on the home page. I made this decision because I figured a main reason people would come to the page would be to see the current beers on tap. I did not want to hide that information. I wanted it to be easily called out and accessible.
